About Food Waste Awareness

Why reducing food waste matters.

What is Food Waste?

Food waste refers to food that is perfectly edible but is discarded at different stages of the food supply chain, from production to consumption. This includes leftovers, expired items, and over-purchased groceries.

Why Does It Matter?

  • Environmental Impact: Food waste contributes to greenhouse gas emissions when it ends up in landfills.
  • Economic Cost: Globally, trillions of dollars worth of food are wasted each year.
  • Social Implications: Millions of people face hunger and malnutrition, yet food waste remains a critical issue.

How Can We Reduce Food Waste?

Here are some practical steps you can take:

  1. Plan Meals: Make a shopping list to avoid buying excess food.
  2. Store Properly: Use appropriate storage techniques to prolong the life of your produce.
  3. Compost: Turn food scraps into nutrient-rich compost instead of throwing them away.
  4. Donate Excess Food: Give surplus food to food banks or shelters.
